The bot is built in Python using Flask as the webhook server. 
When a call is triggered through the /call endpoint, 
SignalWire places a call to the target number (the Pretty Good AI Agent). 
SignalWire was chosen for its low latency and built-in speech-to-text design, which
keep response times natural and conversational. 
Each scenario maps to a Flask route that SignalWire hits as a webhook 
on each turn of the conversation. 
Speech recognition is handled by SignalWire's Gather() function, 
which listens for the agent's response. The transcribed text is passed to OpenAI's GPT-4o mini, 
which generates the patient's next response. 
GPT-4o mini was chosen for its low latency, low cost, and ability to 
maintain a consistent persona across a multi-turn conversation.
The full conversation history is maintained in memory and passed to 
the model on each turn so responses stay contextually coherent. 
When the bot detects a natural conversation ending, it hangs up. 

An automated bug detection function is in place that feeds call transcripts to 
an OpenAI for analysis. These findings are supplementary and should be 
interpreted with discretion, as they may reflect nitpicky observations 
rather than quality issues. 
Bugs I actually found are written in a "Bugs I Found" section.
